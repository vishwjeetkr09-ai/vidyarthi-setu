Business Overview
Vidyarthi Setu is an educational startup based in Nashik, already proven at 3 institutions — now ready to scale.
🌉 The Mission

Bridge the gap between classroom education and real-world career readiness. India produces millions of graduates annually — yet most lack practical skills employers actually want. Vidyarthi Setu fixes that.

🎓 Who We Serve

School and college students (age ~14–22) in Nashik and surrounding Maharashtra districts. Institutions (schools, colleges) are our paying clients. Students are our impact beneficiaries.

🏗️ Business Model

B2B2C: Vidyarthi Setu sells programs to schools & colleges (institutions pay), and delivers directly to students. This gives you scalability — one institution = hundreds of students.

👔 Our Delivery Model

Programs are delivered by working professionals — HR managers, industry leaders, and specialists — not just teachers. This is rare, valuable, and hard to copy.

📍 Current Status

Operational. Already implemented at Don Bosco School, CMCS College, and Galaxy Residential Public School in Nashik. You have proof of concept. Time to scale.

Risks & Mitigations
Know what could go wrong — and have a plan ready.
High
Trainer availability & consistency

Working professionals are busy. If a trainer cancels last minute, it hurts institutional trust. Mitigation: Always have a backup trainer per program. Build a bench of 10+ professionals, not just 2–3.

High
Slow institutional decision-making

Schools and colleges have long sales cycles — approvals may take months. Mitigation: Start building relationships now. Target TPOs and Principals directly. Have a smaller "intro workshop" offer to get your foot in the door.

Med
Dependency on a few key trainers

If your 1–2 best trainers leave or get too busy, quality drops. Mitigation: Document all session content. Build a replicable curriculum so no single person is irreplaceable.

Med
Competition from larger edtech players

WhiteHat Jr, BYJU's, etc. could enter the institutional space. Mitigation: Your local presence, professional trainer network, and existing relationships are hard to replicate at scale from outside Nashik.

Med
Revenue seasonality

Schools have academic calendars — budget decisions happen in April–June. Mitigation: Time your outreach to coincide with new academic year planning (Feb–April). Offer multi-year contracts.

Low
Quality control across multiple trainers

Different trainers = inconsistent student experience. Mitigation: Create a standard feedback form after every session. Monthly debrief with trainer team. Maintain a quality checklist.
